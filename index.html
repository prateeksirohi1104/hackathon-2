About CodeRise 2026

Built by students, run by the Technical Club.

CodeRise 2026 is the flagship hackathon of the Technical Club, organised for the students of Sanskar College of Engineering and Technology, under the Sanskar Educational Group. For 24 uninterrupted hours, teams design, code, and pitch a working solution to a real problem statement.

The event carries the spirit of our institute's own motto — enlightening minds, encouraging creativity, fostering responsibility. It's open to all branches and all years: what matters is the willingness to build.

The Schedule

From the opening bell to closing pitches.

Twenty-four hours, mapped hour by hour — including the sunrise check-in that gives the event its name.

Day One — 20 July 2026
09:00 AM

Reporting & Team Check-in

Teams arrive, verify registration, and settle into their stations.

10:00 AM

Opening Ceremony

Welcome address, rules briefing, and the official problem statement reveal — the 24-hour clock starts here.

11:00 AM

Hacking Begins

Teams start building. Mentors are on the floor for the first round of walk-throughs.

02:00 PM

Mentor Round 1

Get early feedback on your approach before you commit further hours to it.

08:00 PM

Dinner & Reset

A short break to eat, regroup, and plan the overnight push.

11:00 PM

Midnight Code Review

Optional check-in for teams that want a sanity check before the long night stretch.

Day Two — 21 July 2026
05:00 AM

Sunrise Check-in

The moment the arc turns — teams take stock, tie loose ends, and prepare for the final stretch.

08:00 AM

Mentor Round 2

Last chance for guidance before code freeze.

10:00 AM

Hacking Ends

Code freeze. Submissions lock — exactly 24 hours after the opening bell.

11:00 AM

Presentations & Judging

Each team pitches live to the judging panel.

02:00 PM

Results & Closing Ceremony

Winners announced, certificates distributed, and CodeRise 2026 wraps.

Problem Tracks

Pick a lane, or bring your own idea.

Four broad tracks to anchor your build — open interpretation encouraged.

Track 01

Campus & EdTech

Tools that make student life, learning, or college operations noticeably better.

Track 02

Sustainability

Solutions addressing energy, waste, water, or climate at a local scale.

Track 03

Health & Accessibility

Projects that improve wellbeing or make technology usable for everyone.

Track 04

Open Innovation

No brief fits your idea? Build it anyway — this track is wide open.

Why Join

What you walk away with.

Prizes & recognition

Top teams across all four tracks are awarded and featured by the Technical Club.

Mentor access

Two dedicated mentor rounds to sharpen your idea and unblock your build.

Certificates for all

Every participant who completes the 24 hours receives an official certificate.

Organised by the Technical Club

CodeRise 2026 is hosted by the Technical Club of Sanskar College of Engineering and Technology (SCET), an institute of the Sanskar Educational Group. The club runs student-led technical events throughout the year — this is our biggest one yet.
